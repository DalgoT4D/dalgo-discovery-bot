**Short answer: No — Dalgo doesn't replace Salesforce. But it works very well *alongside* it.**

Salesforce is a CRM — it's where your team logs beneficiary records, tracks interactions, manages workflows, and runs programs. Dalgo is a **data platform** — it sits *downstream* of Salesforce, pulling your data out, transforming it, and making it ready for reporting and analysis.

| Layer | Tool |
|---|---|
| Program operations / CRM | **Salesforce** (stays as-is) |

| Transformation / pre-aggregation | **Dalgo's transformation editor** |
| Dashboards / reporting | Looker Studio, Superset, or Dalgo's native dashboards |
